## Description

Zrno turns your iPhone into a precise light meter for analog photography. Point your phone at a scene, and Zrno instantly recommends the aperture and shutter speed for your specific film camera.

MADE FOR FILM PHOTOGRAPHERS

Set up your exact camera and lens combination. Zrno knows your available apertures and shutter speeds, so every recommendation is one you can actually dial in. No more guessing between stops.

FEATURES

- Real-time EV metering using your iPhone camera
- Custom camera profiles with exact aperture and shutter speed values
- Multiple lens support per camera with individual aperture ranges
- Aperture priority and shutter priority modes
- Exposure compensation dial (±3 EV in 1/3 stops)
- All valid exposure combinations at a glance
- Live histogram for scene analysis
- Pixelated camera preview in your chosen color scheme
- Pinhole camera mode with Schwarzschild reciprocity correction
- Film stock presets for accurate long exposure calculation
- Shutter speed calibration for aging cameras
- Multi-camera support (switch between iPhone lenses)
- 4 color schemes: Midnight Noir, Vintage Cream, Frosty Steel, Darkroom Red
- 4 font options: Rounded, Standard, Mono, Serif
- Light, Dark, and System appearance modes

PINHOLE CAMERAS

Zrno is one of the few light meters with proper pinhole camera support. Enter your pinhole diameter and focal length, select a film stock, and get Schwarzschild-corrected exposure times. Supports popular film stocks including HP5+, Tri-X, FP4+, Delta 100/400/3200, T-Max, Acros, Fomapan, and more.

DESIGNED FOR THE DARKROOM

Every pixel of Zrno is themed. Dark mode feels like working under a safelight. Light mode is clean and readable in bright sun. Every color, every font, every element responds to your chosen scheme.

NO ADS. NO SUBSCRIPTIONS. NO TRACKING.

Zrno is a tool, not a platform. Buy it once, use it forever.

## App Review Notes

Zrno requires camera access to function as a light meter. The app measures ambient light using the iPhone camera's exposure metadata (ISO, shutter speed, aperture). No images are captured, stored, or transmitted. The camera feed is processed in real-time on-device to calculate EV (Exposure Value) and generate a low-resolution histogram.

The app contains no in-app purchases, no ads, no analytics SDKs, and makes no network requests. All user data (camera profiles, settings) is stored locally via SwiftData and UserDefaults.

The hidden "Fun Mode" in Settings reveals two simple pixel games (a breakout clone and an endless runner) rendered in the scene preview area. These are cosmetic extras, not the primary function of the app.
